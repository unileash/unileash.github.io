Dark Energy: The Mysterious Force Accelerating the Universe

Dark energy is one of the most profound mysteries in modern cosmology. It is a hypothetical form of energy that permeates all of space and tends to accelerate the expansion of the universe.

  • It accounts for nearly 70% of the universe's total energy.
  • Its presence was first discovered in 1998 during studies of supernovae.
  • Unlike gravity, dark energy causes a repulsive effect on cosmic structures.
  • It is uniformly distributed and does not clump like matter does.
  • We detect its effects indirectly, through the observation of cosmic expansion rates.

Scientists are still unsure whether dark energy is a constant, like Einstein’s cosmological constant (Λ), or whether it varies over time and space. This energy shapes the ultimate fate of the universe, possibly leading to a scenario known as the Big Freeze or even a Big Rip.

The study of dark energy is pushing the boundaries of theoretical physics, and new missions like Euclid and Roman Space Telescope are being prepared to solve this cosmic puzzle.
