Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)

The CMB is the oldest light in the universe — a faint glow left over from the Big Bang, now cooled to just 2.7K. It’s like a baby photo of the cosmos.

  • Discovered: Accidentally by Penzias and Wilson in 1965.
  • Proof of the Big Bang: It supports the theory that the universe began in a hot, dense state.
  • Uniformity: The temperature is nearly the same in every direction, with tiny fluctuations that seeded galaxy formation.
  • Observed By: COBE, WMAP, and Planck satellites mapped the CMB in extreme detail.

Studying the CMB helps scientists understand the early universe, its age, and how matter began to form into the structures we see today.
