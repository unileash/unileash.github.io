⚪ White Holes

A white hole is a theoretical region in spacetime which cannot be entered from the outside, but matter and light can escape from it. It’s essentially the **reverse of a black hole**.

🕳️ The Opposite of Black Holes

While a black hole pulls everything in and nothing escapes, a white hole spits everything out and nothing can enter. It’s like a **cosmic expulsion point**.

📜 Origins in Theory

White holes emerge from the Einstein field equations, the same ones that predict black holes. They are often described as the other side of a black hole's wormhole.

🌌 Could They Exist?

No white hole has ever been observed, but some physicists believe they might form from the death of a black hole or exist in regions of the universe we can't access.

🔁 Black Hole – Wormhole – White Hole?

Some theories suggest that matter entering a black hole might travel through a wormhole and be ejected from a white hole. This has deep implications for **space travel** and **time warps**.

🧪 Problems With the Theory

White holes defy the **second law of thermodynamics**, because they decrease entropy by throwing out ordered matter, which seems physically impossible.

📡 Signals from the Other Side?

Some scientists have speculated that strange phenomena like **fast radio bursts (FRBs)** or gamma-ray bursts could be signs of white holes, but there's no confirmation.

🌠 Final Thoughts

Whether or not they exist, white holes remain one of the most fascinating mysteries in modern astrophysics. They challenge how we think about space, time, and the ultimate fate of matter.
