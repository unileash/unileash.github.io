What is Antimatter?

Antimatter is the mirror image of ordinary matter. For every particle, there exists an antiparticle with the same mass but opposite charge. When matter and antimatter meet, they annihilate each other, releasing massive amounts of energy.

Scientists have observed antimatter in particle accelerators and cosmic rays, but naturally occurring antimatter is extremely rare. The Big Bang should have produced equal amounts of matter and antimatter â€” yet here we are, living in a universe dominated by matter. This is one of the greatest mysteries of cosmology.

  • Antielectrons are known as positrons.
  • Antiprotons carry a negative charge instead of positive.
  • Antimatter has been proposed for use in future space travel.
  • Medical imaging techniques like PET scans use antimatter reactions.

NASA and other agencies have looked into using antimatter as a propulsion fuel due to its energy potential. One gram of antimatter reacting with matter could yield energy equivalent to several kilotons of TNT.
