🚀 Voyager 1 – Humanity's Deepest Explorer

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the farthest human-made object from Earth. It has crossed into interstellar space, over 14 billion miles away, continuing to send back data to Earth!

🛰️ What Is Voyager 1 Doing Now?

Voyager 1 is currently traveling at over 61,000 km/h and still transmitting data about cosmic rays, magnetic fields, and solar wind from outside the solar system.

📀 The Golden Record

The spacecraft carries the legendary Golden Record — a time capsule of Earth sounds, music, and greetings in 55 languages. It's designed to tell alien civilizations who we are... just in case they find it.

🌌 Why Is It Important?

  • 📡 First human probe in interstellar space
  • 🧠 Gave us crucial data about the outer planets and heliosphere
  • 🛸 Represents Earth's legacy to the cosmos

📊 Fun Facts

  • It takes signals from Voyager 1 around 22 hours to reach Earth.
  • The power source is a Plutonium-238 RTG, still functioning after nearly 50 years.
  • It will keep drifting in space for billions of years.
