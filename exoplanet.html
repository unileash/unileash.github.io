🌍 What Are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. These distant worlds have sparked curiosity and imagination, especially with the discovery of Earth-like planets that could potentially harbor life.

🔭 Discovery of Exoplanets

The first confirmed exoplanet, 51 Pegasi b, was discovered in 1995. Since then, thousands have been identified, thanks to telescopes like Kepler and TESS.

  • 🌟 Over 5,000 confirmed exoplanets discovered so far.
  • 🛰️ Methods include transit method, radial velocity, and direct imaging.
  • 🌐 Many exoplanets are part of multi-planet systems.

🌌 Types of Exoplanets

  • 🌍 Terrestrial: Rocky and similar to Earth.
  • 🪐 Gas Giants: Like Jupiter or Saturn.
  • 🔥 Hot Jupiters: Large planets orbiting very close to their stars.
  • ❄️ Ice Giants: Uranus-like planets with icy compositions.

🌱 Possibility of Life

Some exoplanets lie in the habitable zone — the right distance from their star to allow liquid water. These worlds are top candidates in the search for extraterrestrial life.

🧠 Mind-Blowing Facts

  • 🌡️ Some exoplanets are hotter than stars!
  • 🌈 A planet named HD 189733b rains glass sideways in 7,000 km/h winds!
  • 🌊 GJ 1214 b is a water world — possibly an entire planet covered by an ocean.

🚀 Future of Exploration

With missions like the James Webb Space Telescope, scientists are analyzing exoplanet atmospheres for signs of life, oxygen, methane, and even industrial pollutants!

Exploring exoplanets may one day help us find a second Earth or understand how unique our planet truly is.
