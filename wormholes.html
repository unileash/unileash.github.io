What is a Wormhole?

A wormhole is a theoretical tunnel in spacetime that could connect distant parts of the universe. It is like a cosmic shortcut, folding space to reduce the time it takes to travel between two points.

How Wormholes Might Work

Wormholes arise as solutions to the Einstein field equations of general relativity. A traversable wormhole would need exotic matter—a form of matter with negative energy density—to keep it open and stable.

Wormhole Facts

  • Wormholes are often called Einstein-Rosen bridges.
  • No physical evidence of wormholes has ever been found.
  • They could, in theory, allow faster-than-light travel—without violating relativity.
  • Some wormholes may connect distant galaxies or even different universes.
  • Wormholes might be microscopic and exist at quantum scales.
  • Time travel through wormholes is theoretically possible, but not practically achievable with our current understanding.
